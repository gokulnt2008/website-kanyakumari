"Kanyakumari Tourism
The Land Of Three seas"

Kanyakumari is named after the Indian goddess Kumari Amman. It is also called the Land of three seas since the waters of the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal and Indian Ocean join here. It is the southernmost part of India. The Bhagavathy Amman Temple, located at the shores of Kanyakumari has a long history there are many places in Kanyakumari which are worth visiting. The Vivekananda Rock is one such place. The Vivekananda Rock is a massive heavy rock located in the waters of Kanykumari. You can visit there by a boat. There is a big memorial in the rock. Another important place in Kanyakumari is Thiruvalluvar Statue. Thiruvalluvar is the writer of the ancient Tamil poem, Thirukural. The magnificent statue also lies in the waters of Kanyakumari. If you are planning to visit Kanyakumari, then it is better to book Tickets in Nagercoil, it is the nearest city from Kanyakumari or you can also book hotels in Kanyakumari itself. If you are travelling by car then it is better to book a ticket in Nagercoil, where you can fresh up and move to Kanyakumari. Another place you can visit is The Mahatma Ghandi Mandapam. It is one of the place in Kanyakumari were the ashes of Mahatma Gandhi is kept. If you are planning to visit Kanyakumari for about a week, there are a few other places to visit. Thiraparapu Falls is one such place. It is about 50 km away from Kanyakumari. The falls is ideal for kids to play with, and also many trees with it. Thanumalayan is a temple located at the town of suchindram. It is about 13 kilometres from Kanyakumari.
